The following summary covers the most recent updates regarding AIMA, immigration, and related status updates in Portugal as of July 20, 2026.

  • AIMA Case Backlog Progress: A dedicated task force of 28 judges has successfully resolved 22,436 pending immigration cases over the last three months, clearing approximately 18-20% of the total backlog.
  • New Migration Plan: Prime Minister Luís Montenegro recently announced a new migration strategy aimed at tackling the remaining backlog of roughly 400,000 cases, which includes the official termination of the "Manifestation of Interest" mechanism.
  • Automatic NISS Assignment: Starting in late July 2026, immigrant citizens beginning their regularization process at AIMA will be automatically assigned a Social Security Identification Number (NISS), streamlining access to social services.
  • EU Extends Ukrainian Protection: On July 15, 2026, EU countries agreed to extend the temporary protection status for those fleeing the war in Ukraine until March 4, 2028.
  • Military Obligations Clause: Under the new EU agreement, temporary protection will going forward only be granted to new applicants who satisfy their military obligations in Ukraine; this rule does not affect those already holding protection.
  • Temporary Protection Remains Valid: For those already in Portugal, the temporary protection regime continues to provide legal residence, access to work, healthcare, and education.
  • Digital Nomad Visa (D8) Requirements: The D8 visa for remote workers remains active, requiring proof of average monthly earnings over the last three months equal to four times Portugal’s national minimum wage (currently €3,680 per month).
  • Nationality Law Changes: Recent reforms to the Nationality Law in 2026 have introduced changes to naturalization timelines, which may impact how long Digital Nomad visa holders must wait before becoming eligible to apply for citizenship.
  • Online Services for EU Citizens: As of July 2026, AIMA has launched a new online portal allowing EU citizens and their family members to apply for and renew their permanent residence cards online.
  • Ongoing Legal Challenges: Despite improvements in case processing, many foreign residents continue to report technical instability on AIMA platforms and prolonged delays, leading many to seek legal counsel for court injunctions to force action on stalled renewals.
